1. Introduction

1.1 Background

The rapid adoption of Large Language Models (LLMs) in tourism applications—from travel recommendation engines to virtual travel assistants—has created an urgent need for systematic evaluation of their reliability and fairness. Tourism information carries significant real-world consequences: inaccurate destination details can lead to ruined vacations, biased recommendations can perpetuate cultural stereotypes, and unreliable real-time data can disrupt travel plans.

1.2 Problem Statement

AI-powered tourism systems face three critical challenges:

  • Hallucination: Models frequently generate fabricated facts, outdated information, or inaccurate claims
  • Algorithmic Bias: Recommendation systems may disproportionately favor popular Western destinations
  • Real-Time Reliability: Dynamic information such as weather and events cannot be accurately retrieved

1.3 Research Objectives

  • Quantify hallucination rates across multiple LLMs in tourism domain
  • Analyze geographic representation bias in destination recommendations
  • Evaluate real-time information accuracy for dynamic tourism data
  • Provide empirical evidence for risk assessment in tourism AI deployment

2.3 Data Sources and Benchmark Construction

The evaluation benchmark was assembled from a combination of synthetic test prompts and public open-data travel sources to ensure coverage across destinations, transportation, and tourism demand. Synthetic prompts were used for controlled stress-testing of model behavior, while open datasets were used to ground the analysis in real-world travel and mobility patterns.

  • OpenFlights route and airport data: used for airport-network and route-level travel context.
  • Eurostat tourism database: used for accommodation and nights-spent indicators across European destinations.
  • U.S. BTS On-Time Performance data: used for flight punctuality and airline reliability signals.
  • World Bank / UN Tourism indicators: used for cross-country arrivals and macro tourism trends.

Note: Any dataset counts in this report should be kept aligned with the actual files used in the experiment.

3.2 Key Findings

Critical Finding: Real-time information retrieval is severely limited. Only the Llama 3.1 8B model achieved 2.5% accuracy, while all other models failed completely.
Warning: Europe maintains the highest representation (16.2%) across all models, while the Middle East and Caribbean show the lowest representation.
Observation: Destinations in Europe and worldwide contexts receive more positive sentiment (0.28) compared to Africa (0.21) and the Middle East (0.18).

5. Conclusion

5.1 Summary of Findings

This comprehensive evaluation reveals significant challenges in deploying LLMs for tourism applications:

Hallucination: With a 12-14% hallucination rate, tourism AI systems require substantial human oversight.
Geographic Bias: The persistent European dominance in recommendations (16.2% vs 9.8% for Africa) indicates algorithmic bias.
Real-Time Failure: The near-zero accuracy in dynamic information retrieval makes current LLMs unsuitable for real-time tourism queries.

5.2 Recommendations

  1. Hybrid Architecture: Combine LLMs with real-time APIs for dynamic data
  2. Bias Correction: Implement geographic balancing in recommendation algorithms
  3. Human-in-the-Loop: Maintain human verification for factual claims
  4. Continuous Monitoring: Establish ongoing evaluation frameworks
  5. Transparency: Provide source attribution for tourism recommendations

5.3 Future Work

  • Expand evaluation to include more LLMs and providers
  • Develop domain-specific fine-tuned models for tourism
  • Implement user trust studies with controlled experiments
  • Create standardized benchmarks for tourism AI evaluation
